## 2025-02-28 - Cross-Domain Cookie Leakage in yt-dlp Extractor
**Vulnerability:** The functions `filter_youtube_cookies` and `filter_vimeo_cookies` were parsing Netscape HTTP Cookie Files using generic substring checks (`"youtube.com" in line_str`). This would erroneously preserve cookies for malicious domains (e.g., `hacker-youtube.com`) or unrelated files containing the string, leading to potential session hijacking or cookie leakage.
**Learning:** Netscape cookie files are tab-separated. A substring check across the entire line is not safe. Furthermore, standard comments (`#`) and HttpOnly cookies (`#HttpOnly_`) share the same prefix character, so treating all lines starting with `#` as safe comments causes HttpOnly cookies from other domains to be preserved or causes targeted HttpOnly cookies to be skipped.
**Prevention:** Always split lines by tabs (`\t`) and strictly validate the domain column against an exact match or valid subdomain suffix. Explicitly handle `#HttpOnly_` prefixes differently from standard `#` comments.
